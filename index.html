Strasbourg

The cathedral, Petite France, what else? You want to be sure not to miss anything in Strasbourg, even if your stay is short? Here is our selection of the must-see places to visit in the Alsatian capital, for an unforgettable trip!

The Cathedrale

With its 142-meter high spire, the cathedral and its pink sandstone lace dress dominate the entire Alsace plain. Let yourself be seduced by this gothic masterpiece, the symbol par excellence of Strasbourg.

The "Petite France"

Emblematic of Strasbourg, Petite France is the former district of millers, tanners and fishermen. Its half-timbered houses, its small streets and its quays give it a romantic and intimate atmosphere.

The European District

The European district, symbol of the Franco-German reconciliation and the construction of Europe, has some superb jewels of contemporary architecture, including the famous European Parliament.

Paris

Paris has a thousand faces and as many ways to discover it. Must-see sites, unusual and surprising places, key events... Here is our advice.

La Tour Eiffel

Symbol of Paris and more widely of France, the Eiffel Tower was built by Gustave Eiffel for the Universal Exhibition of 1889. It is one of the most visited monuments in the world. From the discovery of the historical gardens to the dazzling view of Paris from the top, discovering the Tower is an incredible experience.

Stroll in the heart of Montmartre

From the Saint-Pierre market to the Abbesses square, discover the very famous Montmartre hillock. Climb to the Sacré-Coeur before going down to the pretty Abbesses district. A village district with cobbled streets, full of secrets and hidden corners.

The Garnier Opera House

A masterpiece of 19th century theatrical architecture, the Palais Garnier, built by Charles Garnier and inaugurated in 1875, is the thirteenth opera house in Paris since the institution was founded by Louis XIV in 1669.

Lyon

From the ancient theaters and numerous vestiges of the Gallo-Roman era to the Old Lyon of the Middle Ages and the Renaissance, from the Presqu'île and its classical buildings to the Croix-Rousse of the silk workers... The city has expanded over time on the banks of the Saône, then beyond the Rhone, to the Confluence of today, while preserving its heritage.

The "Vieux Lyon" District

Vieux Lyon is one of the most emblematic districts of the city: it is essential to visit it if you are in Lyon! Founded in 43 B.C., this district still keeps traces of its rich past.

Les Rives de Saone

Enjoy the calm of the banks of the Saône by walking along the pedestrian promenade where urban and bucolic atmospheres alternate. 15 km of pedestrian walkway close to the water on the left bank, between the Confluence and Ile Barbe and between Fontaines-sur-Saône and Rochetaillée-sur-Saône.

The "Parc de la Tête d'Or"

The "Parc de la Tête d'Or" is one of the largest urban parks in France. A true living cultural heritage, it is the only European park with a place of relaxation and leisure, a botanical and zoological garden, and a rose garden that are entirely free.

La Rochelle

For two, alone or with your family, visit La Rochelle in Charente Maritime. On the program of your escapade: narrow streets with the charm of yesteryear, sea walks, bike and 100% nature outings... without forgetting the local products to discover on the market stalls!

The Old Town

You will love to stroll through the old town, without following a well-defined itinerary. You will inevitably find yourself in front of a historic building that deserves a stop at some point.

The Old Port

How about drinking and eating facing the ocean, or enjoying a good ice cream while strolling along the quays? As the departure point for a sea cruise to discover the coastline and the islands, the Old Port offers a wide range of possibilities and is ideal for a stroll.

The Central Market

Essentially a food market, this unmissable market under the magnificent 19th century market halls is a place of life and a meeting place for the people of La Rochelle.

Marseille

2600 years of history, an unavoidable heritage, jealously preserved traditions and recipes handed down from generation to generation, come and discover the many riches of Marseille.

The creeks of Marseille

The Calanques massif is a natural wonder nestled between Marseille and Cassis. This exceptional and unique landscape is a must-see in Marseille!

The Notre-Dame de la Garde basilica

Emblematic figure of Marseille, it watches over the sailors, the fishermen and all the inhabitants of Marseille. Go and visit the "Good Mother" as she is called in Marseille, and admire the panorama from the top of the hill.

The Orange Velodrome Stadium

An emblematic monument of Marseille, the Orange Velodrome regularly thrills the city during its soccer matches. With friends, family or supporters clubs, come and live a very Marseille experience!

Ajaccio

Visiting the Ajaccio region means discovering authentic villages and breathtaking landscapes.

Capo Di Feno beach

Capo di Feno is a beach near the Gulf of Ajaccio. It shelters one of the most famous surf spot of southern Corsica.

The Genoese district of Ajaccio

It is impossible to know Ajaccio without having visited its Genoese quarter. In the eyes of Ajaccians, the old town is the most beautiful corner of the imperial city.

The path of the crests

In 3 hours in total, the trail of the crests will lead you from the center of Ajaccio to the reliefs of the Bloody Islands. This hike is accessible to all ages. It offers fantastic views of the sea and the Gulf of Ajaccio.
